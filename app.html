FAQ
Common Florida family law questions
An uncontested divorce can be finalized in as little as 3–4 weeks if both parties agree. Contested divorces typically take 6–18 months. Jennifer works to resolve cases efficiently while fully protecting your interests.
Florida law requires a Parenting Plan in any case involving minor children. It details time-sharing arrangements, parental responsibilities, and decision-making for health, education, and welfare.
Yes. Florida allows modifications when there's a substantial, material, and unanticipated change in circumstances — such as job loss, relocation, or change in the child's needs.
Uncontested means both spouses agree on all major issues. Contested means unresolved disputes requiring negotiation or a judge's decision. Having Jennifer review all agreements protects your long-term interests.
Florida uses an income shares model based on both parents' net incomes, number of overnights, and costs for health insurance and childcare. The formula is set by Florida statute, but deviations are possible.
While not legally required, it is strongly advised. Agreements that seem fair now can have serious long-term consequences. Jennifer reviews all documents to ensure your rights are protected.
